Finding an Unequal Wage

In the United States, men earn more than women—quite a bit more, as it happens. Currently, the “gender wage gap” is about 78 cents to the dollar—that is, for every dollar a man earns, on average, a woman earns only 78 cents. (The gap is about the same for only full-time workers.)

Women and the Pay Gap

Happily, some professions are more equal than others. To the leftAbove are the and for each of the Census’ industry categories. For example, Community and Social Services, Healthcare Support, and Office Administration are staffed mostly by women, and pay them better than average—though, still not equally to men.

On the other side, industries with very few women, like Construction and Maintenance tend towards equality as well.

Far and away the least equal industry is the Law—which is all the more alarming as it is also the highest earning.

Data from the American Community Survey 2013, 5-year estimates.
